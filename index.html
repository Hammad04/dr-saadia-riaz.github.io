What is a Rheumatologist?

A rheumatologist is a physician who is qualified in the diagnosis and treatment of arthritis and other diseases of the joints, muscles and bones.

What do Rheumatologists treat?

Rheumatologists treat arthritis, certain autoimmune diseases, musculoskeletal pain disorders and osteoporosis, including rheumatoid arthirities, osteoarthiritis, gout, lupus and back pain.

When you should see a Rheumatologist?

If you are suffering from severe and persistent joint or muscle pain, you should consult the rheumatologist. It's important to determine a correct diagnosis early so that appropriate treatment can begin as soon as possible.
